Repetition & Alignment

A website that I think is a good example of repetition is Tsuki. Image shown is from a cellphone, and it may not be a good preview of what their website looks like, but you'll see what I mean when you visit their website.

  1. Tsuki is simple and straightforward to navigate on, with 2 logos on each side, as well as the images seen at the center. 4 images having shown with the same sizing for repetition.
  2. Sign of repetition with the image sizing doesn't end there, as navigating to their collection leads you to their products for sale. They are also aligned evenly on their organization of products, with the help of grids.
  3. Additionally, they will also show a repetition of design and alignment for their categories on the collection. This makes their website easy to look at and distinguish categories from their grid of products.

Proximity


Bron Bron Land?
- You heard that right. Bron Bron Land. This is an example for Proximity, mainly because of how the website makes you go through Lebron's history.

What do you mean by that?
- The website starts you with showing the whole Bron Land, then you click or scroll from the "Start" area.
It zooms in and practically gives you a tour, going around Bron Bron Land.
As it moves you around the land, you also get a text to read from the left side of the screen, as you take a closer look at the Bron Bron Land.

So as you proceed through each tourist spot of Bron Bron Land, you get a glimpse of Lebron's history?
- Essentially, yes. While you're given a block of text on one side of the screen, it is not too big to keep you engaged on the visual side.
The proceeding tourist spots help keep your confusion to a minimum when engaging on Bron Bron Land.
That is what makes this an example of Proximity.
